<p><p>Orphan diseases, also known as rare diseases, are medical conditions that affect a small percentage of the population. These diseases often have a genetic basis and are characterized by their low prevalence, which is typically less than 200,000 individuals in the United States or less than 5 in 10,000 individuals in the European Union. Due to their limited prevalence, orphan diseases are often overlooked in terms of research and development, making it challenging for patients to access effective treatments or care.</p><p>However, in recent years, there has been a growing focus on orphan diseases, driven by increasing awareness and supportive regulatory initiatives. The Orphan Drug Act passed in the US in 1983 and similar regulations in other countries have incentivized pharmaceutical companies to invest in the development of orphan drugs, which are specifically designed to treat rare diseases. These regulations provide various benefits such as exclusive marketing rights, tax incentives, and extended patent protection for orphan drugs, encouraging innovation in this field.</p><p>The orphan diseases market has experienced significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue expanding. The market growth is driven by factors such as the rising prevalence of orphan diseases, advancements in medical technology and research, and increasing government support. Additionally, the shift towards personalized medicine and targeted therapies has also contributed to the growth of this market.</p><p>The market is witnessing several trends, including a shift towards precision medicine approaches, where treatments are tailored to the specific genetic characteristics of individual patients. This approach is made possible by advancements in genetic sequencing and molecular diagnostics. Another key trend is the increasing use of innovative technologies such as gene therapy, cell therapy, and RNA-based therapies in the treatment of orphan diseases. These therapies offer potential curative solutions for rare diseases by directly targeting the underlying genetic abnormalities.</p><p>In conclusion, the orphan diseases market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 11.9% during the forecast period.
