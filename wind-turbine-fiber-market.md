<p><p>Glass fiber and carbon fiber are two main types of fibers used in wind turbine manufacturing. Glass fiber is more commonly used due to its lower cost and good mechanical properties, making it suitable for blades and other structural components. On the other hand, carbon fiber is stronger, stiffer, and lighter than glass fiber, but it is also more expensive. Carbon fiber is often used in high-performance applications where weight reduction and superior mechanical properties are required, such as in the aerospace industry.</p></p>
