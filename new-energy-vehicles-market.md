<p><p>New Energy Vehicles (NEVs) are categorized into three main types: Hybrid Electric Vehicles (HEVs), Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs), and Electric Vehicles (EVs). HEVs combine a traditional gasoline engine with an electric motor to improve fuel efficiency. PHEVs have a larger battery capacity than HEVs, allowing for longer electric-only driving ranges. EVs run solely on electricity stored in a battery, with no gasoline engine. Each market type caters to different consumer needs and preferences for sustainable transportation solutions.</p></p>
