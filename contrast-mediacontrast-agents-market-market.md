<p><p>Contrast media, also known as contrast agents, are substances used to enhance the visibility of internal body structures in medical imaging. There are different types of contrast media including Iodinated, Gadolinium, Barium, and Microbubble. Iodinated contrast media are commonly used in CT scans, while Gadolinium is used in MRI scans. Barium is often used in X-rays to highlight the digestive system, and Microbubble contrast agents are used in ultrasound imaging. Each type of contrast media serves a specific purpose in improving the quality of medical images.</p></p>
