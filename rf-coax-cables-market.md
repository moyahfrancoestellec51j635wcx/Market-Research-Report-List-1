<p><p>RF coax cables are classified into different types based on their flexibility and rigidity. Semi-rigid type cables have a solid outer conductor, providing high stability and low signal loss. Semi-flexible cables offer a balance between rigidity and flexibility. Flexible cables have a braided outer conductor, allowing for easy bending and installation. Other types of cables may include conformable, hand-formable, and low-loss options, each catering to specific requirements in terms of performance, cost, and durability in various applications.</p></p>
