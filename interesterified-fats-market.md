<p><ul><li>Chemical Interesterified Fats</li><li>Enzymatic Interesterified Fats</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Interesterified fats are a type of modified fat created by rearranging the fatty acids in a triglyceride molecule. Chemical interesterified fats involve the use of chemical catalysts to rearrange the fatty acids, while enzymatic interesterified fats use enzymes to catalyze the reaction. Both types of interesterified fats are used in food manufacturing to improve the texture, stability, and shelf life of products. Chemical interesterified fats are more commonly used due to their lower cost and faster reaction times, while enzymatic interesterified fats are seen as more natural and environmentally friendly.</p></p>
