<p><p>Bamboo fibers can be categorized into two main types: Natural Bamboo Fiber and Chemical Bamboo Fiber. Natural bamboo fiber is derived from the bamboo plant through mechanical or physical processes, retaining the natural properties of bamboo such as antibacterial and moisture-wicking abilities. On the other hand, Chemical Bamboo Fiber is produced using chemical processes to break down the bamboo plant into fibers, resulting in a softer texture and improved dyeing capabilities. Both types of bamboo fibers are used in various industries such as textiles, paper, and construction.</p></p>
