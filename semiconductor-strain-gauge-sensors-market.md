<p><p>Semiconductor strain gauge sensors are used to measure and detect mechanical strain or deformation in objects. There are two main types of market variations for these sensors: naked gages and backed gages. Naked gages refer to sensors that are directly attached to the test object using adhesive materials. This type of sensor is suitable for applications where space is limited. On the other hand, backed gages are mounted on a flexible substrate, which provides mechanical support. Backed gages are preferred when a larger area for strain measurement is required or when the sensor needs additional protection from environmental factors.</p></p>
