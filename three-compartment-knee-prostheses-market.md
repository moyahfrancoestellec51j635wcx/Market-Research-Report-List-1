<p><p>Fixed-bearing, mobile-bearing, and fixed or mobile-bearing are three types of knee prostheses available in the market. Fixed-bearing prostheses have a stationary plastic insert between the femoral and tibial components. Mobile-bearing prostheses have a polyethylene insert that moves slightly within the metal components. Fixed or mobile-bearing prostheses allow for flexibility in the surgical procedure, as the decision to use a fixed or mobile insert can be made during the surgery. Each type has its own advantages and potential drawbacks, depending on the patient's individual needs.</p></p>
