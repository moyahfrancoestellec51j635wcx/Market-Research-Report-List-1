<p><p>Primary antibodies are antibodies produced by a host animal in response to an antigen. They bind specifically to the target antigen, making them useful for detecting and quantifying proteins in samples. Secondary antibodies, on the other hand, are antibodies raised against the primary antibody's host species. They bind to the primary antibody and are often used to amplify signals in experiments such as Western blotting or immunohistochemistry. Both primary and secondary antibodies play crucial roles in various research and diagnostic applications.</p></p>
