<p><ul><li>Friction Type</li><li>Tooth Type</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Electromagnetic single disc clutches are classified into three types based on their mechanisms: friction type, tooth type, and others. Friction type clutches use friction between the disc and the clutch plate to engage and disengage, providing smooth operation. Tooth type clutches rely on interlocking teeth on the disc and plate for engagement. The other types of clutches may use different mechanisms such as magnetic attraction or electrostatic force to transfer torque. Each type offers unique advantages and is suited for specific applications.</p></p>
