<p><ul><li>N-type Semiconductor</li><li>P-type Semiconductor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Extrinsic semiconductors are formed by introducing impurities into a pure semiconductor material, altering its electrical properties. N-type semiconductors have impurities called donors, which provide extra electrons, resulting in excess negative charge carriers. This creates a material with high electron conductivity. Conversely, P-type semiconductors have impurities called acceptors, which create electron "holes" in the material. These "holes" act as positive charge carriers. Market-wise, both N-type and P-type semiconductors are essential in various industries, including electronics, telecommunications, and renewable energy, playing crucial roles in devices like transistors, diodes, and solar cells.</p></p>
